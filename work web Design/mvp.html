  1. Uganda: Ugandan Shilling (UGX)

    2. The Ugandan Shilling is the official currency of Uganda, introduced in 1987 to replace the East African shilling. It is subdivided into 100 cents. The currency symbol is "USh."   2. Rwanda: Rwandan Franc (RWF)

    3. The Rwandan Franc is the currency of Rwanda, introduced in 1964. It is subdivided into 100 centimes, with the symbol "RF." The National Bank of Rwanda manages the currency. The franc has undergone various changes and is crucial for the country’s economic transactions and trade.

  3. Tanzania: Tanzanian Shilling (TZS)

    4. The Tanzanian Shilling has been the official currency of Tanzania since 1966, replacing the East African shilling. It is divided into 100 senti, with the symbol "TSh." The Bank of Tanzania oversees the issuance and regulation of the shilling, which plays a vital role in the country's economy.

  4. United States: United States Dollar (USD)

    5. The United States Dollar is the official currency of the United States and one of the most widely used currencies in the world. Introduced in 1792, it is symbolized by "$" and is subdivided into 100 cents. The USD serves as the world's primary reserve currency and is used in international trade and finance.

Euro: Euro (€)

The Euro is the official currency of the Eurozone, used by 19 of the 27 European Union countries. Introduced in 2002, it replaced various national currencies. The Euro is subdivided into 100 cents and is symbolized by "€." The European Central Bank manages the Euro, which is the second most traded currency globally.

Kenya: Kenyan Shilling (KES)

The Kenyan Shilling is the currency of Kenya, introduced in 1966, replacing the East African shilling. It is subdivided into 100 cents, with the symbol "KSh." The Central Bank of Kenya is responsible for issuing and regulating the shilling, which is crucial for domestic and regional trade.
