COMPUTERS ILLITERATES

Computer illiterates refer to individuals who lack basic knowledge and skills to use computers and other digital technologies.

THEIR CHARACTERISTICS

  • Limited or no experience with computers.
  • Difficulty in understanding basic computer concepts.
  • Struggle to use basic computer applications.
  • They feel intimidated when using computers.
  • Often rely on others for technical support.

Causes of computer illiteracy

  • Lack of access to computers and technology.
  • Limited education in computer skills.
  • Age-related barriers.
  • Learning disabilities.
  • Poverty.

Challenges faced by Computer illiterates

  • Difficulty in accessing information and services online.
  • Limited job opportunities due to lack of computer skills.
  • Social isolation from digital communities.
  • Vulnerability to online scams and cyber threats.
  • Frustration when trying to use computers.

Solutions

  • Basic computer training programs.
  • Access to computers and internet in public libraries.
  • Online resources and tutorials.
  • One-on-one mentoring and support.
  • Inclusive and accessible technology design.

Benefits of digital Literacy

  • Improved employment opportunities.
  • Enhanced access to information and services.
  • Increased social connections and community engagement.
  • Better online safety and security.
  • Improved overall quality of life.
